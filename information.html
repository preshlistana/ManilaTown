INFORMATION


How did the protests start?

Due to the urbanization of low-income neighborhoods in SF, many people were forced to be evicted in multiple areas, but especially in the I-Hotel. Luckily, due to the 1968 protest, we were allowed to stay for 3 more years.


Why are we protesting now?

Sadly, our lease is over and they’re once again forcing evictions on us. We will not stand for this, we will not move. The I-Hotel is one of the last places standing in Manilatown due to urbanization. We want our Manilatown back, we want to keep our homes. These evictions are cruel and should not be happening. Remember: “No Evictions! We Won’t Move!


Who can we contact to end this?

You can contact your district representatives through phone or letter. You can also contact the mayor at + 1 (415) 554-4000 or 1 Dr Carlton B Goodlett Pl, San Francisco, CA 94102. Please speak to them about the current injustice going on in Manilatown. If you need help with calls or letters, you can check out the templates below or on the home page.
