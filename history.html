HISTORY


The history of Manilatown and the International Hotel:

The Filipino community in the U.S started when many Filipino men (they weren’t allowed to bring family) immigrated to the U.S for more opportunities. In San Francisco, 30,000 Filipinos started creating a neighborhood (now known as Manilatown) on a 10-block strip of land bordering Chinatown. Outside of this area, it was hard for Filipinos to find housing due to racism. They ended up living in residential hotels such as the International Hotel. Sadly, due to the ”Manhattanization” of Manilatown, many of these areas are now gone. But we will continue to fight for the International Hotel.
